# Security+ - Study Roadmap
---
## The 10-Day Study Plan
### **Day 1: General Security Concepts (12%)**
- CIA Triad and security fundamentals
- AAA (Authentication, Authorization, Accounting)
- Zero Trust Architecture principles
- Security controls: preventive, detective, corrective
- Change management and policy documentation
- PKI, certificates, and cryptography basics
- Practice: Baseline MCQ test to gauge starting level

### **Day 2: Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations (22%)**
- Threat actors: motivations, capabilities, and profiles
- Attack vectors:
  - Phishing and social engineering
  - Supply chain compromises
  - Insider threats
- Vulnerability types:
  - Zero-day exploits
  - Misconfigurations
  - Unpatched systems
- Malware indicators and behaviors
- Mitigation strategies:
  - Network segmentation
  - Patch management
  - Application whitelisting
- Practice: ~75 MCQs covering Domain 2

### **Day 3: Security Architecture (18%)**
- Enterprise infrastructure:
  - Firewalls and IDS/IPS
  - VPNs and secure remote access
  - Load balancers and proxies
- Cloud and hybrid architecture models
- Data protection strategies:
  - Encryption at rest and in transit
  - Tokenization and masking
  - DLP solutions
- High availability and disaster recovery:
  - RAID, clustering, and replication
  - Backup strategies and restoration testing

### **Day 4: Security Operations - Part 1 (28%)**
- Vulnerability management:
  - CVSS scoring and prioritization
  - Scanning tools and schedules
  - Remediation workflows
- Monitoring and detection:
  - SIEM platforms and log collection
  - Behavioral analytics and UEBA
  - Alert tuning and false positive reduction
- Asset management and inventory
- Secure configuration baselines and hardening

### **Day 5: Security Operations - Part 2**
- Identity and Access Management:
  - MFA and SSO implementations
  - IAM frameworks and lifecycle management
  - Privileged Access Management (PAM)
- Incident response phases:
  - Preparation and identification
  - Containment, eradication, recovery
  - Post-incident activities
- Automation and orchestration in SOC
- Digital forensics fundamentals:
  - Evidence preservation and chain of custody
  - Memory and disk analysis basics
- Focus: Heavy MCQ practice across Domains 4 & 5

### **Day 6: Security Program Management (20%)**
- Governance, policies, standards, and procedures
- Risk management:
  - ALE and quantitative risk assessment
  - Risk treatment strategies
  - Risk registers
- Third-party and vendor risk management
- Compliance frameworks:
  - GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS
  - ISO 27001 and NIST
- Audit types and reporting
- Privacy concepts and data classification

### **Day 7: PBQ & Simulation Focus**
- Cyberkraft PBQ practice sessions
- Free simulation labs:
  - Log analysis and interpretation
  - Firewall rule configuration
  - System hardening walkthroughs
  - Network configuration review
- Approach: Attempted each lab independently before reviewing solutions

### **Day 8: Mixed Practice Exams**
- Two full-length (90-question) timed practice exams
- Simulated real exam conditions:
  - Time pressure
  - No distractions
  - Mixed domain questions
- Post-exam analysis:
  - Identified weak areas by domain
  - Flagged topics for targeted revision
- Targeted revision using the official exam objectives PDF

### **Day 9: Light Review & Teach-Back**
- Techniques:
  - Flashcards for acronyms and key concepts
  - Teach-back method: explaining topics aloud to reinforce understanding
  - Whiteboard diagrams for processes (IR lifecycle, PKI flow)
- Focus Areas:
  - Weak domains (vulnerability management, compliance)
  - Quick MCQ refresh across all domains

### **Day 10: Final Review & Exam Strategy**
- Final practice exam: one more timed run-through
- Exam strategy review:
  - Elimination technique for narrowing down options
  - Question flagging and time allocation
- Preparation checklist:
  - Exam logistics confirmed
  - ID and confirmation documents ready
  - Light warm-up questions only
- Mental preparation: stayed calm, confident, and well-rested

## The Study Approach Breakdown
I started with General Security Concepts since they form the foundation of everything else. CIA Triad, AAA, Zero Trust, security controls, PKI, and change management are the building blocks you keep referring back to. Once that was solid, I took a baseline MCQ test to see where I actually stood.

Then I spent a solid day on Threats and Vulnerabilities. This is the biggest domain (22%), so I went deep on threat actor motivations, attack vectors like phishing and supply chain, and how to identify IoCs. Around 75 MCQs that day helped lock in the key distinctions between similar-sounding threats.

Day 3 was Security Architecture. I reviewed enterprise infrastructure (firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPNs), cloud and hybrid models, and data protection methods. Encryption, tokenization, and high availability concepts are heavily tested here, so I made sure to understand the "why" behind each mechanism, not just the "what."

Days 4 and 5 covered Security Operations, which is the heaviest domain at 28%. I split it in two: Part 1 focused on vulnerability management, SIEM and monitoring, and secure baselines. Part 2 covered IAM, incident response, automation, and digital forensics. I did heavy MCQ practice throughout these two days because this domain has a lot of scenario-based questions.

Day 6 was Security Program Management. Governance, policies, risk management, compliance frameworks, and audits. I paid extra attention to ALE calculations and the differences between GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS since those come up a lot.

Day 7 was all about PBQs and simulations. I used Cyberkraft for PBQ practice and did free simulation labs on log analysis, firewall rules, and system hardening. I made a point to attempt each one independently before looking at solutions—that helped me understand what the exam expects in terms of practical thinking.

Day 8 was two full-length practice exams under timed conditions. The point wasn't to ace them but to find my weak spots. After each exam, I went back to the exam objectives and did targeted revision on the areas I struggled with.

Day 9 was a lighter review. I used flashcards for acronyms and key terms, and the teach-back method to explain concepts out loud. If I couldn't explain something clearly without looking at my notes, I knew I needed to revisit it.

Day 10 was the final review. One more practice exam to stay sharp, a review of exam strategies like elimination and flagging, and making sure all the logistics were sorted. The key was staying calm—going in with confidence and knowing I'd put in the work.

## Exam Tips & Strategy
### 1. Read Questions Backwards
Start from the last line of the question. The actual ask is usually there. Then read the scenario with that context in mind. It stops you from getting lost in the details.

### 2. Eliminate First, Compare Second
Don't hunt for the perfect answer. Actively eliminate the wrong ones first, then compare what's left against the question. "Does this actually answer what's being asked?" is the question to keep asking.

### 3. Watch for Domain Keywords
Words like "first step," "BEST," "MOST likely," "LEAST invasive" change what the answer should be. "First step" usually points to detection or identification. "BEST" means the most effective or comprehensive option. These keywords point you to the right domain and concept.

### 4. Think Like an Analyst
Every question has a real-world angle. Ask yourself: "If I were on a SOC team and saw this, what would I actually do first?" That grounds abstract concepts and makes the answer obvious.

### 5. Flag and Move On
If a question takes more than 30-45 seconds, flag it and move on. Don't get stuck. Many questions become clearer after you've warmed up on others. You can always come back to flagged questions if time allows.
